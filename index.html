The summary of Mohammed bin Abdulrahman bin Jassim Al-Thani’s visit to Thailand

February 21, 2020February 21, 2020| Janice RobinsonJanice Robinson| 0 Comment
The private sector is now being encouraged to cooperate with Qatar as part of its Look East policy, a nation exploring business partnerships with Thai partners on energy, infrastructure, public health, hospitality, and trade. “Qatar’s sovereign wealth fund has also shown interest in investing in Thai stocks, while the Thai government has proposed that Qatar ramp up its investment in Thailand as the gateway to Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, and Vietnam,” said Thai Deputy Prime Minister. Deputy Prime Minister Somkid Jatusripitak met with Sheik Mohammed bin Abdulrahman bin Jassim Al-Thani, Qatar’s Deputy Prime Minister and Bangkok’s Minister of Foreign Affairs, and discussed how the two countries intend to work together on trade and investment in energy, construction, and the Eastern Economic Corridors. Qatar has raised $338 assets worth around the globe enabling its sovereign wealth fund to become the 14th largest in the world. Prime Minister Prayut Chan-o-cha has also given the Qatari foreign minister a call. Mr. Somkid intends to lead the Investment Board, Economic Ministers, and members of the business sector at a visit to Qatar sometime this year. Qatar is Thailand’s third-biggest trading partner in the Middle East and Thailand’s 24th largest trading partner on the world market. Two-way trade in 2018 amounts to $3.91 billion, up 38.6 percent from the previous year. Exports to Thailand totaled $379.1 million, with imports worth $3.52 billion. Qatar’s investment in Thailand also totaled $4.32 million in 2018, while Thai investment in Qatar amounted to $609 million in 2018. Qatar is the primary source of energy for Thailand, which receives 7 percent of its total energy imported from Qatar. In 2018, Thailand’s Qatari visitors numbered 35,838 and were the most sought after of health services. Qatar is keen to set up branches at Thai hospitals in Qatar, as the nation is seeking to become a Mideast Medical Centre.
